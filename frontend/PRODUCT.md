# Termorize Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Mixed audience of language learners — from casual commuters fitting in practice between tasks, to dedicated students with specific goals, to polyglot enthusiasts who care about precision. They open the app inside Telegram, often in short sessions. The interface must work for all of them without over-explaining or over-entertaining. The job to be done is simple: save words, practice them, remember them.

### Brand Personality
**Precise. Steady. Understated.**

The brand doesn't perform enthusiasm — it earns trust by being consistently useful. Like a well-made tool: no ornamentation, no friction, just confidence in the craft. The green palette signals growth and language, not cheerfulness.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Reference:** Notion, Linear — clean, dark, tool-first. Minimal decoration, high information density where it matters.
- **Anti-reference:** Duolingo — avoid gamification chrome, confetti, streaks, loud rewards. No cartoon energy.
- **Theme:** System-matched. Both light and dark modes are equally polished; neither is an afterthought.
- **Visual tone:** Quiet and crisp. Ample whitespace, but not sparse. Typography-led since the product IS words.
- **Green:** The existing green palette (HSL ~142°) is right — treat it as a grounding neutral, not a brand shout. Use it to accent, not flood.

### Design Principles

1. **Tool first, decoration last.** Every visual element must earn its place. If removing it doesn't hurt clarity or function, remove it. Ornamentation is a debt against focus.

2. **Typography is the product.** Words are the core content. Font hierarchy, weight contrast, and spacing are the primary design medium. Text must be always comfortable to read — size, leading, and color contrast are never negotiable.

3. **Confidence over celebration.** Progress should feel real and earned, not performed. Avoid animated confetti, streak counters, or loud positive reinforcement. A clean success state is enough.

4. **Precision at every touch point.** Interactions should feel deliberate and immediate. Tap targets are generous, transitions are subtle (not flashy), and feedback is instant. In a Telegram mini-app context, the user's finger is the cursor.

5. **Quiet green.** The green palette is a signal, not a statement. It should feel like the natural background of the interface — present, calm, purposeful. Reserve higher-saturation greens for meaningful actions (primary CTA, success states). Everything else lives in muted tones.
